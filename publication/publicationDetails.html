The struggle for Police Reforms

"The Struggle for Police Reforms" talks about a critical analysis of the Indian police system and the need for reforms. It highlights the lack of accountability, politicization, and inadequate training and resources in the police force, resulting in a failure to protect citizens' rights. Singh recommends structural changes such as depoliticization, separation of investigation and law and order functions, and citizen participation in police oversight. The book also discusses the landmark Supreme Court directives, including the famous "Prakash Singh vs. Union of India" case, which mandated states to implement police reforms. Overall, the book serves as a wake-up call for policymakers and citizens to demand a more accountable, professional, and citizen-centric police force.

author name:Meena Nayyar & Himmat Singh Shekhawat
